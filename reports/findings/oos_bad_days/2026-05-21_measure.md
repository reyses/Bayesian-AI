## 3. Does the B-stack help or hurt on bad days?

On the 14 FLAT-negative days:

- FLAT mean $-255/day -> stack mean $-80/day.
- **Paired delta on bad days: $+175/day** (95% CI [$+98, $+269]) -- SIGNIFICANT.
- Stack improved 12/14 bad days, worsened 2/14, unchanged 0.
- Bad days the stack flipped to POSITIVE: 6/14.
- Total bad-day $ : FLAT $-3,570 -> stack $-1,120 (recovered $+2,450).

- Worst single FLAT day $-571; under the stack the worst day is $-544.
- For context, on the 37 good days the stack delta is $-9/day (CI [$-112, $+91]).

**Read:** the B-stack is net-positive on bad days in point estimate (it cuts/skips some losing legs), but the per-day delta CI behaviour determines significance -- see number above. The stack does NOT reliably turn bad days good; it shaves the loss.

### Eyeball summary

Strongest separators (by |standardized gap|):

- **frac_winner_legs** is lower on bad days (bad 0.3219 vs good 0.4358, corr -0.68).
- **n_legs_perday** is lower on bad days (bad 45.14 vs good 62.27, corr -0.29).
- **leg_amp_std** is lower on bad days (bad 22.42 vs good 29.02, corr -0.22).
- **overnight_range_pct** is lower on bad days (bad 0.0103 vs good 0.01406, corr -0.20).
- **atr_pts** is higher on bad days (bad 7.688 vs good 6.51, corr +0.21).

Caveats for the ANALYZE phase:

- n is small (51 days, ~14 bad). Single-feature correlations here are noisy -- treat as hypotheses, not findings.
- Several leg-structure features (leg_pnl_std, worst_leg_usd, frac_winner_legs) are partly mechanical consequences of a bad day, not causes -- do NOT use them as a same-day selector signal. Cross-day and pre-open features (overnight gap, prior-day range, VIX, day-of-week, event flags) are the legitimate ex-ante levers.
- The 11 Sunday sessions confound weekday analysis: they are short (low n_legs) and lack cross-day features. Check whether bad days concentrate in short low-leg-count sessions vs full sessions.
