### Volatility = std(velocity)
Standard deviation of price change per unit time. Distribution of a first derivative.
At different TFs it measures different things:
- std(dP, 1h) = trend amplitude (the envelope)
- std(dP, 1m) = noise amplitude (the tradable chop)
- ratio = noise-to-trend = how much is tradable

### Variance Ratio = Brownian Test
- Ratio = 1 → random walk (no edge)
- Ratio > 1 → trending (momentum)
- Ratio < 1 → mean-reverting (fade)

Replaces Hurst AND ADX with one grounded measurement.

### ADX: Standard Because First, Not Best
Wilder 1978. Double-smoothed ratio of directional movement / total range.
Answers "is it trending?" — same as variance ratio, but through 5 layers of abstraction.
Standard because it was published first and built into every platform.

### DMI Restored
DMI measures buyer/seller battle (highs vs lows), different from velocity (close-to-close).
Individually weak (r=0.006), but DMI extreme + volume collapse = exhaustion signal.
Kept as: dmi_diff (grounded derivative) + dmi_volume_exhaustion (cross-signal).
