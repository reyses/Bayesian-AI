## PROPOSAL — CRM-as-substrate for standalone_research

### Current behavior
`standalone_research.py` runs Analysis L/M/N etc. on RAW PRICE at a chosen TF.
Primitives extracted are raw-price segments (5-level shape library).

### The reframe
Feed **CRM SERIES** instead of (or alongside) raw price:
- 15s CRM as input → primitives describe 15s-CRM shape behavior
- 1m CRM → 1m-CRM shape behavior
- 5m CRM → 5m-CRM shape behavior
- 15m CRM → 15m-CRM shape behavior

Each CRM is a SMOOTHED VIEW of price. Their shape language is different:
- Raw 15s price shape "STEEP_LINEAR_UP" = price went up fast
- 15s CRM shape "STEEP_LINEAR_UP" = the 15s MEAN itself is rising fast → sustained directional move
- 15m CRM shape "FLATLINE" = "CRM slowed down" (what user described 2026-05-12)

### Why this matters
The user's mental model integrates the LINES (CRMs) over multiple timescales,
not raw price. Picks like #20 (z_15m=+0.52, MFE $714) had no z-extension but
showed "bands compressed + CRM slowed down" — a SHAPE-of-CRM signal that's
invisible to point-in-time z lookups.

### What CRM-shape primitives capture (not visible in current substrate)

| Primitive | What it means |
|-----------|---------------|
| `15m_CRM_shape = FLATLINE` after `STEEP_LINEAR_UP` | CRM rolling over — bow forming |
| `5m_CRM_shape = STEEP_LINEAR_DOWN` after `FLATLINE` | sleeping pivot → impulse |
| `1m_CRM_shape = STEEP_CONCAVE_UP` while `15m_FLATLINE` | local recovery within range |
| `15s_CRM_curvature` flipping while `15m_slope ≈ 0` | cusp at the top of a flat range |

These are TIME-SERIES SHAPE features that point-in-time anchors (z, slope at one bar)
cannot represent. The user's eye reads them naturally; the substrate doesn't.

```

  - Input: M_15s / M_1m / M_5m / M_15m series instead of raw OHLC
  - Outputs: shape labels per segment per CRM-level
  - Primitive chord at each bar:
       (15s_CRM_shape, 1m_CRM_shape, 5m_CRM_shape, 15m_CRM_shape)

```
