## Why the smoothing hurt

The state-machine sticky behavior commits to a direction during transitions
when raw is correctly abstaining. The "low-confidence-but-committed" bars
(strength < 0 bucket for smoothed: n=12,580 bars at 57.8% acc) are the
biggest drag.

The 3-hour windowed-EMA cutoff fixed the stale-state concern but the inertia
itself was the problem, not infinite-history bias.

## Evening — composite trade-management experiments

Continued the session by building the full state-machine composite
(B1+B4+B5+B6 + trajectory) and testing it across THREE operational
frames: exit timing, position sizing, entry filtering.

### Models added late session

- **B4 pivot-region** (symmetric ±W window): W=300s thr=0.85 → 79% prec
  at 10% coverage. Beats B1 K=10 thr=0.85 at 2.5× more coverage.
- **B5 leg-phase** (3-class EARLY/MID/LATE): 38.6% OOS accuracy
  (below MID-baseline 46.4%). High-conf P(MID)>0.60 gives 71.4% prec
  at 0.25% coverage. Weak standalone, useful in composite.
- **B6 directional pivot** (3-class NO/LONG/SHORT): K=10 thr=0.70 →
  53.6% prec (LONG) / 56.7% prec (SHORT) at 5% coverage. Real
  directional ML edge — first model that tells us WHICH direction
  the next pivot will flip.

### Composite probability cloud (Bohr-style state machine)

`tools/pivot_probability_cloud.py` composites all 5 classifiers + trajectory
into 8 action zones: AT_PIVOT, NEAR_PIVOT, IMMINENT, NEAR_3m, NEAR_5m,
WIDE_ZONE, WATCH, CLEAR. Plus trajectory state (RISING/FLAT/DECAYING).

Calibration: 57.7% of bars CLEAR (low pivot risk), 27% in pivot-near
zones, 15.6% WATCH. Pivot probability ranges from 7.5% (CLEAR+B5_MID
>0.50) to 79% (NEAR_PIVOT/IMMINENT cluster).

### Three operational tests — exits FAIL, sizing WINS

| Frame | Best result | Verdict |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Trail tighten (naive) | -$44/leg | ❌ |
| Trail tighten (B6 + hysteresis sweep, 18 configs) | -$0.29/leg best | ❌ 99% loss closed but doesn't beat zero |
| Target placement (12 configs) | -$17.93/leg best | ❌ |
| **Sizing aggressive** | **+$10.97/leg vs flat, +$626/day** | **✅** |
| Entry suppression (any) | +$6 to +$68/leg | ⚠️ but per-day total drops |

**Structural insight**: R-trigger exits at `peak - R`. Any tighter
exit needs to predict the peak precisely, which V2 features can't.
So trail-tightening and target-placement are structurally hopeless
under current signal precision.

### The actionable finding: variable sizing works

Entry-time composite SIGNALS predict leg quality (counter-intuitively):
- AT_PIVOT entries: $+121/leg (2.2× baseline)
- IMMINENT entries: $+86/leg
- CLEAR entries (baseline): $+54/leg

Aggressive sizing scheme (2.0× on AT_PIVOT or B6≥0.70, 1.2× on near-pivot,
0.8× on weak, 0.0× on CLEAR+B6<0.50):
- 1528 legs taken, 299 skipped
- +$81/leg (vs $70 baseline), +15.6% lift
- $4,626/day vs $4,000 baseline
- Per-day bootstrap CI overlaps with flat (NOT statistically significant at N=32 days)
- But all 32 days positive under every scheme — direction is consistent

### Entry suppression is a trap

All binary skip filters reduce per-day total even as per-leg improves.
The skipped legs have small-but-positive expectation; dropping them
costs more than the quality lift adds. Variable sizing (smaller on
weak, not zero) wins because it captures the contribution of every leg
proportional to its predicted strength.

### Caveats

1. All P&L numbers assume oracle entry timing (enter AT the pivot).
   Realistic deployment subtracts ~$32/leg for R-trigger lag.
   Relative improvements should hold; absolute numbers are too high
   by ~50%.
2. N=32 OOS days. Day-bootstrap CIs overlap. The sizing improvement
   isn't statistically significant at p<0.05, just directionally
   positive across all days.
3. The R-trigger baseline already captures +$70/leg. Sizing adds
   ~15%. No tested approach beats this without different exit logic
   entirely (regime-aware exits, time-stops with gating, partial fills).

### Operational recommendation if going live

1. Use variable sizing per entry composite signal (not entry skipping)
2. Don't bother with trail tightening or target placement
3. R-trigger remains the exit
4. Multipliers: AT_PIVOT 1.5×, IMMINENT 1.5×, NEAR_PIVOT 1.2×,
   WATCH 1.0×, CLEAR 0.8×; B6≥0.70 +0.5× bonus; hard skip only on
   CLEAR + B6<0.50
