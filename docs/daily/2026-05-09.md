## Key conceptual locks

```
TERMINOLOGY
  pivot ≡ inflection ≡ bar where direction changes
  reversion = the new leg that begins at the pivot/inflection
  leg = continuous directional run between two pivots
  trend-follow / ride = trade WITH the move
  reversion trade = trade taken at pivot, captured during leg

3-BODY ENVELOPE (always under tension, no force-free equilibrium)
  M_close (blue)  = center / target / close-volatility center
  M_high  (green) = upper bar-extreme regression mean
  M_low   (red)   = lower bar-extreme regression mean
  Each has its own σ envelope (±1, ±2, ±3)
  
GOLDILOCKS LEVELS
  ±1σ — too common (≈32% of bars), no edge
  ±2σ — ★ PRIMARY trigger (≈5%, real reversion edge)
  ±3σ — extreme (≈1.5%), often regime-shift / continuation
  M_close ±3σ — outer wall (rare), highest-conviction reversion confirmation
  
MIXED-REGIME BANDS
  HL anchor = SLOW TF (15m, 1h, 4h)  →  rare extreme triggers
  CRM anchor = FAST TF (1m, 5m)      →  responsive target
  Asymmetric TF design separates "when to fire" (slow, rare)
  from "where to aim" (fast, achievable).
  At 1h-HL: P(continuation past ±2σ) ≈ 0 — reversion is ~certain.

THREE TEMPORAL SCALES
  Scale 2 (RUN)       single ±3σ run at 1h: median 15s, q90 ~8 min, max ~56 min
  Scale 3 (IMPULSE)   multiple connected runs, 1-3 hours, with intervening retraces
  Scale 4 (STABILIZE) σ_rank returning to baseline, ~30 min
  
COMPRESSION-PRECEDES-EXPANSION (1h, ±3σ events, IS 50 days)
  T-60 min:  σ-rank median 0.42  ← compressed (below baseline 0.55-0.6)
  T-30 min:  σ-rank median 0.52  ← expansion begins
  T-15 min:  σ-rank median 0.63
  T-5  min:  σ-rank median 0.57  ← elevated, near event
  T   0:     3σ extreme begins
  ↑ this gives ~30-60 min lead time on early-warning compression
```

### z_high / z_low investigation

Initially tried plotting z_high/z_low as price-space bands using
`band = mean + z_high * sigma`. Misinterpreted them as "high band of regression."

**Corrected**: z_high and z_low are z-scores of the bar's HIGH and LOW
(not band positions). The proper "regression with bands" is mean ± k·σ.
The z_high/z_low are useful as numeric per-bar signals with constant ±1/±2/±3σ
reference lines (SPC interpretation), not as price bands.

### Volume = regime-change confirmation, NOT direction

Volume vol_mean / vol_sigma SPIKE during the rollover (14:00) AND during
the crash (16:00). Both spikes look IDENTICAL — volume is direction-agnostic.

**Operational rule (locked in memory)**: never use volume as a buy/sell trigger

REGIME gate ("event day vs normal day").

### Volume velocity + acceleration = LEADING pre-pivot signal (NEW)

Visual finding on 2026_02_12 around the 14:00-14:30 rollover:

```
ALL FOUR volume-derived features spike simultaneously at the pivot:

  vol_mean (panel 2)     →  green curve explodes upward
  vol_sigma (panel 3)    →  expansion confirming
  vol_velocity (panel 8) →  sharp spike — rate of volume growth jumping
  vol_accel (panel 9)    →  positive spike — rate-of-rate accelerating
```

`vol_velocity` and `vol_accel` are the LEADING indicators because they
capture the RATE OF CHANGE of volume, which rises BEFORE volume itself
plateaus at a new high level. The sequence:

```
T-N min:  vol baseline                    ← normal regime
T-K min:  vol_velocity rising              ← INSTITUTIONS START
T-1:      vol_accel spiking                ← acceleration of participation
T   0:    vol_mean explodes                ← spike now visible
          PIVOT begins                      ← price direction flips
T+:       σ expanding                       ← impulse develops
```

**Multi-signal pre-pivot pattern**:

```
1. σ-rank compression (T-30 to T-60min)     bands tightening
2. vol_velocity rising (T-N min)             rate of volume growth jumping
3. vol_accel spiking (T-1 min)               second derivative of volume
4. CRM slope flattening (T-K to 0)           plus our existing detector
5. CRM slope flips (T = 0)                   pivot bar
6. vol_mean / σ explode (T+)                 confirmation phase
```

When MULTIPLE signals from independent channels (volume, σ, slope) align,
pivot probability is much higher than any single signal in isolation.

**Implication for the macro-event detector**: the 5-min confirmation window
after a candidate pivot should include vol_velocity / vol_accel as
confirmation signals alongside σ_rank rising. If volume is also surging,
the pivot is real; if volume is flat, it's a wiggle.

Refined volume rule: volume IS direction-agnostic for WHICH WAY price
will go — but vol_velocity / vol_accel are direction-agnostic LEADING
indicators of WHEN a pivot is about to happen.

## STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS — MUST be in every layer

**Time-of-day awareness** — the framework MUST condition on time-of-day in EVERY
probability lookup. Reversion edge, impulse probability, σ-rank baselines, and
band-collapse ratios all vary systematically across the trading session:

```
Session phase             Reversion edge         Impulse probability
────────────────────      ──────────────         ──────────────────
Asia / overnight          modest                 low
EU pre-open               modest                 medium (open-anticipation)
US pre-market (06-09 ET)  moderate              medium-high
US OPEN (09-10 ET)       LOW (impulses)         HIGH (open volatility)
US morning (10-12 ET)     HIGH (range)           LOW
US lunch (12-13 ET)       HIGHEST (chop)         LOWEST
US afternoon (13-15 ET)   moderate              medium (afternoon trends)
US close (15-16 ET)       LOW (impulses)         HIGH (close auction)
Post-close                low                    medium (overnight roll)

The probability table CANNOT be flat. Each (state, regime, hurst, sn) bin
needs a TIME-OF-DAY axis layered on top:
   key = (state, regime, hurst, sn, hour_bucket, day_of_week, calendar_event)
```

**Current-day awareness** — the framework MUST track what's already happened
TODAY before deciding what's likely next:

```
Yes-impulse-already → expect stabilization, reversion CAN work
No-impulse-yet      → impulse probability rises throughout day if compression building
Multiple-impulses   → range-bound day; reversions work in each leg
Quiet all morning    → US OPEN about to fire; pre-position trend-follow

Day-state machine running across the session:
  pre-session → open-impulse → midday-range → afternoon-drift → close-impulse
  
Each transition shifts which strategy regime is active.
```

**Calendar-event awareness** — FOMC days, NFP days, CPI days, earnings have
DRAMATICALLY different distributions. They are scheduled-impulse days:

```
On scheduled-event days:
  Reversion is gated to AFTER the event releases
  Pre-event = compression peaks; reversions fail (wait-and-see)
  Event-time impulse is stochastic — direction unpredictable
  Post-event reversion has highest edge once initial spike completes

Need a calendar-event flag (manual or auto) to tag days for differential treatment.
```

We have `L0_time_of_day` already as a V2 feature (captures intraday position).
Day-of-week from timestamp. Calendar event needs external data feed.

These conditioning axes MUST be in every probability table from the start —
adding them later changes ALL the bin populations and invalidates earlier work.

## CRM detector v2 built and tested — sobering result

`tools/crm_pivot_detector_v2.py` extends v1 with:
- New `PIVOT_CANDIDATE` state with bounded monitor window (default 5 min)
- Triple-confirmation gate inside the window:
  1. New slope sign holds for ≥ 60 s (no whip-back)
  2. σ-rank rises to ≥ 0.65 (band expansion)
  3. vol_velocity rank rises to ≥ 0.65 (the LEADING signal)
- Suppresses unconfirmed candidates as wiggles
- New `STABILIZING` state re-arms only after slope rank returns to baseline
- Forward-pass clean — bar-by-bar, no future data

### Results

| Day | Candidates | Confirmed | Suppressed | 14:30 macro pivot? |
|-----|-----------|-----------|------------|--------------------|
| 2026_02_12 | 18 | 7 | 11 | **NO — suppressed** ("no sigma-rise") |
| 2026_03_03 | 17 | 4 | 12 | partial — 15:20 caught afternoon leg |

Charts: `chart/2026_02_12_crm_pivot_v2.png`, `chart/2026_03_03_crm_pivot_v2.png`.

### What v2 caught and what it missed

**Caught (2026_02_12)**: 06:05 / 07:05 / 09:00 / 12:05 / 12:45 / 13:09 / 23:35
— mostly micro-pivots in calmer regimes (morning chop, lunch reversal, midnight chop).

**Missed (2026_02_12)**: 14:30 — the famous macro crash. Suppressed because
σ-rank didn't cross 0.65 within 5 min of the candidate firing.

### Why v2 missed the very pivot it was built to catch

σ-rank uses a 60-min lookback. On the gentle 14:00-14:30 rollover σ stayed
compressed (the same compression-precedes-expansion pattern we validated
empirically). σ didn't actually elevate until ~14:35-14:45 when the
explosive leg developed — past the 5-min monitor window.

v1 caught 14:30 because v1 had **no timeout** — it waited indefinitely for
σ to rise. v2's monitor window over-suppresses slow-buildup macro events.

### Two detection regimes, one gate is not enough

```
MICRO-IMPULSE  fast-confirm path  σ + volvel cross 0.65 within 5 min
MACRO-PIVOT   slow-confirm path  σ trajectory rises monotonically over 15-30 min
```

A single threshold can't distinguish "wiggle that lasted 6 min" from "macro
pivot whose σ takes 15 min to spool up". Real fix is either:
- **Two-path confirmation**: separate fast (5 min, threshold-cross) and slow
  (15-30 min, slope-of-σ-rank rising) paths, both feeding the same gate
- **σ trajectory**: replace single-threshold-cross with monotonic rise over
  the monitor window — a 0.45 → 0.55 → 0.62 series confirms even though no
  bar exceeds 0.65
- **Volume-leading-only fast confirm**: vol_velocity/vol_accel spike was
  visible BEFORE the 14:30 σ-rise; a vol-only fast-confirm path may catch
  what σ-based confirm misses

### Status

This is a **finding**, not a fix. The reversion-suppressor we wanted —
"don't fade into a 14:30-style 3 hr crash" — currently still fires at 14:30
because v2 suppresses the warning. Next iteration must address the two-regime
problem before macro-gate goes into FadeAtBand.

### Z-definition correction (important)

Initially used V2's `L3_z_high` = bar_high vs M_high — but discovered this
NEVER reaches |z|>=3 at any TF (max ~2.6 across 345 days). The V2 z is

ratio stays bounded. **Switched to the operationally-correct definition**

## LIVE-TIME ARCHITECTURE — Bayesian table replaces oracle

User closed the loop (verbatim): "the probability table is since we
cant use oracle live we can use a bayesian probability table to look
up the primitives".

```
RESEARCH PHASE                       LIVE PHASE
==============                       ==========
ORACLE                          -->  BAYESIAN TABLE
(post-hoc, full event known)         (lookup at-bar)
                                     keyed by primitive bucket vector
                                     output = expected outcome / P(_)
                                     no lookahead
```

### How this composes with what already exists

The codebase ALREADY has Bayesian-table infrastructure:

  core/bayesian_brain.py              probability table + direction learning
  nn_v2/book.py                        Bayesian leaf + versioned book + evolution CSV
  core/timeframe_belief_network.py    11-TF worker consensus

These were built around different conditioning axes (NMP entry-tier
features, regime classifier features, 79D CNN outputs). The 2026-05-09
work ADDS a new conditioning vector: the 15m-CRM primitive vector

      x(t) = (slope_q, curv_q, z_close_q, sigma_rank_q, r2adj_q)

Each component is a small-cardinality bucket index (q=quantile id).
The full vector x(t) is a primitive 'chord' — the joint key into the
table — which is computed at-bar with no lookahead.

### Live-time loop

At each 5s bar:
  1. Compute the 5 primitive features (forward-pass clean, already
     proven in `tools/event_bucket_15m_crm.py`)
  2. Bucket each into its quantile bin
  3. Look up the joint cell P(...) in the Bayesian table
  4. Decide via the existing context-filter framework:
        fire = entry AND filter_1(...)  AND filter_2(...) AND ...
     where filter_i is "this bucket's expected outcome / P(_) is
     above the tier's required threshold"

### What the V0 build now produces

V0 must produce two artifacts:
  1. A LOOKUP TABLE indexed by bucket vector, returning
     {n_events, P(continuation_60m), expected_max_z, expected_duration,
      expected_$/trade_per_tier}. JSON or parquet for fast load.
  2. A SIGN-STABILITY REPORT showing which axes survive IS->OOS so
     we know which buckets to TRUST in live.

Translation-table additions:
  oracle           = post-hoc retrospective analysis with full event
                     resolution (max_z, final duration, MFE/MAE,
                     PnL of any tier evaluated against the event)
  Bayesian table   = lookup keyed by primitive bucket vector,
                     returns conditional outcome statistics, used
                     at-bar in live deployment as the substitute
                     for oracle knowledge
  primitive chord  = bucket-vector at a single bar — the joint key
                     into the Bayesian table

## THE BAYESIAN TABLE IS A META-ROUTER (not just one filter among many)

User: "which tells us the probability of being in a macro event cascade
where everything fails and the only two options is ride the wave or
bow out".

This articulates the critical OUTPUT of the table. Reframed:

```
At each 5s bar:
  x(t) = primitive chord = (slope_q, curv_q, z_close_q,
                             sigma_rank_q, r2adj_q)
  P_cascade(t) = P( bar t is in a macro-event-cascade | x(t) )

  if P_cascade(t) is HIGH:
      reversion-style tiers (FADE_*, FadeAtBand, NMPFade) are
      structurally going to lose money — DISABLE them.
      Branch to:
        (a) RIDE — fire trend-follow tiers (RIDE_AGAINST, RIDE_CALM,
                    RIDE_MOMENTUM) that EAT the cascade direction
                    instead of fighting it
        (b) BOW   — no trade, sit on hands

  else:
      normal tier-filter cascade applies:
        fire = entry AND filter_1 AND filter_2 AND ...
```

The Bayesian table is therefore a TWO-LEVEL gate:

```
Level 1 (meta-router):    {RIDE, BOW, NORMAL} based on P_cascade(t)
Level 2 (context filter): per-tier filter cascade as locked earlier
```

Level 1 is a NEW concept that didn't exist in the codebase before.
The context-filter architecture from earlier today applies AT level 2.
Level 1 is what the deterministic CRM v2 detector was TRYING to be —
but as a probabilistic lookup it succeeds where the state machine
over-suppressed (the 14:30 macro pivot was rejected by v2's 5-min
σ-rank threshold; in the Bayesian table its primitive chord would
already have been seen many times in IS and the conditional
probability would correctly route to RIDE/BOW regardless of σ-rank
timing).

### What this does for the V0 deliverable

V0 produces per primitive-chord-bucket:

  n_events                   how many IS events landed in this chord
  P_cascade                  fraction that resolved as macro-event-cascade
                              (criterion: e.g. duration > 30min OR max_z >= 4)
  P_continuation_60m         fraction that extended past 60min
  expected_max_|z|           median peak deviation reached
  expected_duration_min      median run length
  per_tier_oracle_$/trade    {FADE_CALM: $, FADE_MOMENTUM: $, RIDE_AGAINST: $, ...}
                              the failure-mode signal — which tiers bleed,
                              which thrive, in this chord

Three rule-of-thumb thresholds derive from this:

  P_cascade > P_high         -> Level-1 = RIDE (long trend-follow tier)
  P_cascade > P_low          -> Level-1 = BOW
  else                       -> Level-1 = NORMAL (delegate to Level-2)

P_high and P_low come from calibration: choose values where the
expected $/trade of (RIDE | P > P_high) > 0 and where the worst tier
in the chord still survives below P_low.

Translation-table additions:
  cascade           = a chord whose conditional resolution is
                       'macro event' (sustained move >= threshold);
                       reversion-style tiers structurally fail here
  ride the wave     = trade WITH the dominant slope direction
                       (use RIDE_* tiers, target trail-stop exit)
  bow out           = no-trade decision (size = 0)
  meta-router       = Level-1 gate that selects which tier family is
                       eligible to fire based on P_cascade(t)

## ARCHITECTURAL LOCK — context filter vs tier

User: "agree the bad signal and CRM are context filters not a tier in
on its self".

Locking the distinction:

```
TIER             a strategy that fires ENTRIES with a direction
                 (FADE_CALM, KILL_SHOT, RIDE_AGAINST, FreightTrain, etc.)

CONTEXT FILTER   a gate/conditioner that decides WHEN tiers should fire
                 (4 robustness filters, CRM macro detector, regime label,
                  hurst, swing_noise, time-of-day, calendar event)
```

Tiers PRODUCE trade signals. Filters CONDITION which signals are allowed
through to execution. They compose multiplicatively:

```
Strategy = Entry rule  AND  filter_1  AND  filter_2  AND  ...
         = (5s touches band)  AND  (hurst<0.60)  AND  (require_divergence)
                              AND  (CRM not in macro-impulse)
                              AND  (regime != DOWN_SMOOTH)
                              AND  ...
```

When I tested FadeAtBand "with its filters" as a single tier I conflated
two things. The correct verdict on today's iso run is:
- ENTRY RULE (5s touches 15m ±2σ → fade to 5m mean) is rejected
- The 4 ROBUSTNESS FILTERS survive as reusable context components
- The CRM macro detector is a context filter (not a strategy on its own)

### Implications for future work

1. **Test entry rules in isolation** (no filter contamination) so we know
   the rule's edge before bolting filters on.
2. **Test filters in isolation** (apply same filter to multiple entry
   rules; measure incremental contribution) so we know each filter's
   marginal value.
3. **Build a filter LIBRARY** — each filter is a reusable predicate.
   New convention: tiers under `strategies/`, filters under `filters/`
   (directory to be created when first reused).
4. **The pending first-passage probability table is ALREADY a filter
   grid** — every axis (state, regime, hurst, sn, tod, dow, cal_event)
   IS a context filter, and the cell value is the fully-conditioned
   probability. A "tier" is a named selection of filter values.

   forward), NOISE following STEEP_LINEAR_DOWN runs 35.5% UP (i.e. 64.5%
   continues DOWN) on n=9,539. Mirror image: NOISE following
   STEEP_LINEAR_UP runs 62.2% UP on n=9,793. Very tight CIs. Trend
   continuation in the immediate aftermath is a real, measurable, large-n
   effect at the smallest scale.

2. **FLATLINE-after-rally is bullish** — at motif level (5m, 30min
   forward), FLATLINE following STEEP_LINEAR_UP runs 74.6% UP. Same
   pattern at sub_motif (64.6%) and note (mirror image). The market
   pauses, then resumes. Compression-precedes-expansion in shape form.

3. **Acceleration-during-trend is bullish** — STEEP_CONCAVE_UP within
   STEEP_LINEAR_UP at sub_motif level: 68.4% UP (n=321). This is the
   "accelerating uptrend within larger uptrend" pattern.

4. **Counter-trend curves fail** — STEEP_CONVEX_UP inside GENTLE_CONCAVE_UP
   at sub_motif: 24.5% UP (i.e. 75.5% DOWN). A small accelerating up
   inside a larger decelerating up means the larger pattern is exhausting.

5. **CIs tighten dramatically at deeper levels** — phrase n=2k spreads
   CI ±0.15 at best, while note n=10k tightens to ±0.005. The substrate
   is statistically dense at the bottom.
