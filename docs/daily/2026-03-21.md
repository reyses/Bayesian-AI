### Architecture Decisions
1. **Only ONE cumulative field existed**: term_pid (cumsum of z_scores). Everything else
   (F_momentum, DMI, ADX, velocity, volume_delta) was already per-bar or rolling window.
2. **Sensor gate should be proportional**: absolute thresholds don't scale across regimes.
   Z-score relative to rolling stats auto-calibrates.
3. **Fake peak filter belongs on EXIT side, not ENTRY**: high vol+fm at entry = good entry.
   High vol+fm DURING trade = the move hasn't peaked yet, hold.
4. **The counterfactual engine is the goat brain**: every trade and skip spawns phantom
   trades with alternative parameters. Optimal settings emerge from real-time what-if.

### Multi-TF Pivot Seed Scanner (NEW RESEARCH)
Built `tools/pivot_seed_scanner_mtf.py`: detect pivots at 1s, confirm with 1m, context from 15s.
Ran on ATLAS_1WEEK: 4,718 pivots (4,626 real, 60 fakeouts).

**Key findings -- REAL vs FAKEOUT discriminators:**

1m confirmation (move exhaustion):
- s1m_F_momentum: REAL=+38, FAKE=-35 (fakeout has 1m momentum AGAINST reversal)
- s1m_volume_delta: REAL=+23, FAKE=+36 (fakeout has MORE volume, move not done)
- s1m_dmi_diff: REAL=-1.0, FAKE=-2.7 (fakeout has stronger DMI trend)

15s context (entry state):
- s15_F_momentum: REAL=-16, FAKE=-92 (fakeout = entering against freight train)
- s15_dmi_diff: REAL=-0.9, FAKE=+1.2 (fakeout DMI trends WITH old move)

Cross-TF agreement (the killer signal):
- vol_agreement: REAL=0.61, FAKE=0.77 (both TFs agree on volume = fakeout)
- fm_agreement: REAL=0.60, FAKE=0.73 (both TFs agree on momentum = fakeout)

**Conclusion: TF agreement = continuation, NOT reversal. Disagreement = real reversal.**
This validates the 1m sensor gate and the cascade fade exit research.
