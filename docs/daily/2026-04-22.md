## Cycle 1 — Cohen d at RM pivots (STANDARDIZED)

**Hypothesis**: journal's Cohen d=−2.46 claim reproduces on current ATLAS.

**Result**: **PASS**. All 3 success gates cleared.
- Pooled IS (R=$4): |d| = **1.96**
- Pooled OOS (R=$4): |d| = **1.95** (Δ = 0.01, signal survives OOS pristine)
- Monthly std: **0.16** (gate < 0.8)
- Direction accuracy when rule is flipped: **83.5%** (matches journal's 86%)

**Surprise**: my initial direction rule was **inverted**. At a HIGH pivot, residual is NEGATIVE (mean −0.99), not positive as mean-reversion would suggest. Physics: 60-bar OLS lags a turning price; at a peak, RM keeps rising while price stalls → residual goes negative.

**Corrected rule**: trade the pivot direction. LOW → LONG, HIGH → SHORT.

## Cycle 2 — Naive forward pass (ADJUST)

**Hypothesis**: using Cycle 1's rule + next-pivot exit nets ≥ $10/trade.

**Result**: **ALL 7 gates failed.** 
- R=$4 mean $/tr = **−$2.10** (gate was +$10)
- Trade WR (PnL ratio) = **−0.06** (gate was ≥ +0.5)
- Day WR = 42% (gate was ≥ 60%)

**R sweep** (all negative except barely-positive R=$10):

| R | $/day | $/tr mean | $/tr median | tradeWR | DayWR |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $2 | −$21 | −$1.08 | −$16 | −0.04 | 45% |
| $4 | −$27 | −$2.10 | −$20 | −0.06 | 42% |
| $6 | −$7 | −$0.74 | −$23 | −0.02 | 48% |
| **$10** | **+$7** | **+$1.06** | **−$25** | **+0.02** | **50%** |
| $15 | −$50 | −$9.17 | −$31 | −0.15 | 45% |
| $20 | −$114 | −$24.72 | −$40 | −0.31 | 42% |

**Root cause (analyze)**: 2R retracement tax (entry + exit confirmation) consumes leg amplitude. At every R, mean leg is barely above 2R → mean trade ≈ $0.

**Key insight**: Cohen d at pivots measures direction accuracy (which is real), but direction accuracy ≠ tradeable edge. The zigzag geometry taxes us twice per trade.

Captured only **0.24%** of RM cord ceiling at best R (target was 20–30%, so 80–120× away).
