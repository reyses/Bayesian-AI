# 2026-04-21 — Cord ceiling reframe + direction-prediction reality check

## TL;DR

- **Regression cord = $2.5K/day IS, $2.9K/day OOS** — the honest ceiling, 30% of the price cord (70% is intra-bar noise).
- Direction prediction from the 91D feature set at any bar = coin flip (max |d|=0.01 across 2.87M events, single-feature + polynomial + z-stratified all agree).
- Direction prediction at zigzag pivots via regression residual (`1m_z_se`) = **86% oracle accuracy** (Cohen d = -2.46 walk-forward stable, strongest signal found in 2 weeks of research).
- **The oracle accuracy doesn't execute.** Forward pass with 1s slippage drops to negative. 1m-close resolution was masking intra-bar whipsaw that kills tight SLs in 1-2 seconds.
- NMP-baseline captures 2.3% of OOS regression cord. 10x headroom exists physically.
- Next: build forward pass on **regression-line pivots** (not price pivots) to catch smooth-trend legs while ignoring noise.

## Research arc today

### 1. Iso pipeline diagnostic suite

Ran the full distribution analysis on iso_is.pkl (277 days, 5,268 trades, +$86K) and iso_oos.pkl (68 days, +$4,755).

- **Segment diagnostic** (tools/tier_segment_diagnostic.py): IS edge concentrated in Q2 2025 (+$46K of +$86K total). Q3 = +$2.5K (dead quarter). OOS Q1 2026 = $67/day. Most tiers show DECAY or FLIP patterns across segments — not monotonic overfit, but event-driven edge.
- **Daily concentration** (tools/tier_daily_concentration.py): top-10 days carry 100-400%+ of each tier's total. April 9 2025 alone = $17K+ across tiers. April 2-11 cluster = $35-40K of the $86K IS total. The engine is an event harvester, not a consistent earner.
- **Mode analysis** (tools/pnl_tier_distribution.py --source iso): IS overall mode is bimodal (65 days <-$500, 65 days >$1K tied). No typical day. OOS skews to `-$500:-$200` (25%) as mode.
- **NMP coverage** (tools/nmp_coverage.py, inline): NMP fires 15/day IS, 39/day OOS. That's 5-13% of available $15-leg universe. NMP holds median 22min, uses 4 chain slots with 32% utilization.

Conclusion: iso engine has room to grow. The question is WHICH direction.

### 2. $15/8min movement tagging + direction prediction

Built `tools/tag_15_movements.py`. 2.87M events across 277 IS days — **87% of bars had $15 movement within 8 min**. Mean bars-to-first = 25 (~2 min). Hours 13-20 UTC (9am-4pm ET) are the 95%+ saturation window.

Ran `tools/movement_direction_eda.py` (phase 1 + phase 2):
- **Phase 1** (single-feature Cohen d across 91 features): max |d| = 0.010. ZERO walk-forward stable features for LONG-first vs SHORT-first direction prediction.
- **Phase 2** (polynomial expansion on top 6 features → 27 feature combos): 0/27 walk-forward stable. Polynomial products don't rescue the direction signal.
- **Z-stratified** (`tools/movement_z_stratified.py` @ 5 TFs × 7 z_se bins, with 1m-boundary sampling): max walk-forward bias = 3pp at deep-z extremes. Mean reversion exists but weak.

**Hard conclusion**: direction at any bar is information-theoretically random in the 91D feature space. Volatility (dead-zone detection) is predictable (`5m_bar_range` d=+0.82); direction is not.

### 3. Cord length — price vs regression

Built `tools/cord_length_1m.py` and `tools/cord_length_regression.py`.

**Price cord @ $15 threshold:**
- IS: $8,375/day (160 legs/day)
- OOS: $10,285/day (196 legs/day)

**Regression cord @ $15 threshold (60-bar OLS window):**
- IS: $2,504/day (20 legs/day)
- OOS: $2,874/day (21 legs/day)

**Efficiency (reg/price) = 27-30%**. Price cord is mostly noise. The regression cord is the honest ceiling for any strategy that relies on underlying trend signal.

Capture:
- NMP IS: $311 / $2,504 reg = **12.4% of regression cord**
- NMP OOS: $67 / $2,874 reg = **2.3% of regression cord**

OOS has 40x physical headroom vs current NMP.

### 4. Zigzag-pivot residual — the strongest statistical signal

Built `tools/regression_line_cohen_d.py`. At each zigzag pivot (R=$15), compute regression-line features and Cohen d between UP-next vs DOWN-next cohorts.

| Feature | d_IS | d_OOS | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `res_10_norm` | **-2.460** | **-2.400** | Dominant. UP-next has deeply negative residual (price below mean). |
| `res_20_norm` | -2.019 | -1.899 | Same at 20-bar window |
| `res_60_norm` | -1.179 | -1.133 | Same at 60-bar |

14 walk-forward stable features, all residual-family. |d| > 2.0 on multiple features. That's 246× the signal-to-noise of raw bar-level features.

**Classification accuracy**: 86.8% IS / 86.0% OOS at zigzag pivots — the highest direction-prediction number we've ever produced on this data.

### 5. S/R context, wick, volume, velocity stratification

Built `tools/pivot_accuracy_stratified.py`. Layered multiple signals:

- **Chord ratio (reg_chord/price_path_length)**: STRONG_TREND regime → 77% IS / 72% OOS (baseline was 68/65).
- **Rejection wick** at pivot bar: STRONG (>50%) → **84% IS / 80% OOS**.
- **Volume ratio** at pivot bar: SPIKE (>2×) → **79% IS / 74% OOS**.
- **2D wick × volume**: STRONG wick × ELEVATED vol → **90% IS / 89% OOS** (n=811 IS / 225 OOS).
- **Velocity stratification (the biggest finding)**:
  - AGAINST-prediction price_vel → **81% IS / 78% OOS** (26k IS pivots / 82% of sample)
  - WITH-prediction price_vel (strong) → **8% IS / 8% OOS** — flip this direction → 92% right

### 6. Oracle pivot-residual sim (pivot lookahead intentional)

Built `tools/pivot_residual_sim.py`. Entry at each zigzag pivot at `close[pivot_bar]`, direction from `res_10_norm` sign, TP/SL/inverse exits.

- Best config: TP$15 / SL$10 / inv_thr=2.0 → **+$1,358/day IS / +$1,664/day OOS**
- 95% WR, median hold 2 bars
- This is the physics upper bound IF you could enter at the exact pivot extreme (you can't — pivot confirmation is retrospective).

### 7. Forward pass @ 1m — the middle result

Built `tools/pivot_residual_forward.py`. Real-time pivot detection with $5 retracement confirmation. Entry at confirmation bar's close (NOT true pivot — costs $5/trade).

TP×SL sweep winner: **TP$50 / SL$3 → IS +$750/day, OOS +$897/day**, 22% WR (but 16.67:1 RR). 9% cord capture, walk-forward balanced.

Looked good. Wasn't real.

### 8. Forward pass @ 1s — reality check

Built `tools/pivot_forward_1s.py`. SAME strategy but exits resolved at 1s bar granularity using intra-bar high/low.

**Result: -$265/day IS / -$248/day OOS at TP$50/SL$3.** 96% SL hits at median 1-2 seconds hold time.

The 1m-close resolution was smoothing out intra-bar whipsaw. At 1s granularity, the SL triggers before price can develop toward TP. Wider SLs don't fix it — they just extend losers. Velocity filter + flip made it WORSE (-$722 IS).

**The 1m sim overstated performance by ~3x.** The pivot-residual strategy is NOT viable at realistic execution granularity.

### 9. Regression cord as honest ceiling (the reframe)

The 1s slippage reality gap had a number: price cord ($8-10K/day) vs regression cord ($2.5-3K/day). The ~$7K/day delta is pure intra-bar noise that cannot be extracted.

Realistic target is regression-cord-based strategies:
- Entry at regression-line pivots (not price pivots)
- Hold through the full regression leg
- Wider SL absorbing price noise
- Fewer, longer trades

## What we proved

1. Direction at arbitrary bars = coin flip (confirmed 3 ways)
2. Direction AT ZIGZAG PIVOTS via regression residual = strong Cohen d = 2.46 **but requires pivot lookahead**
3. Price cord ($8-10K/day) is 70% noise; regression cord ($2.5-3K/day) is the real ceiling
4. 1m-close sims are ~3x optimistic vs 1s intra-bar reality
5. NMP at 2.3% of OOS regression cord has 40x headroom

## What we disproved

1. "Pivot-residual with TP$50/SL$3 captures +$750/day" — that was a 1m-close artifact
2. "The 91D feature set predicts direction" — not at any bar, any z bin, or any polynomial combo
3. "5-day S/R levels add independent signal" — they add nothing on top of residual
4. "Volatility-adaptive SL helps" — fixed $3 SL beat all vol-adaptive variants
