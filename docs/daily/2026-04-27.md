## Bugs found

### 1. v1.3-RC: `Position.AveragePrice` stale during multi-TF backtest
**Symptom**: 391 of 1,047 trades (37%) exited at exactly `entry ± 5pt` ($11.90 loss with commission) despite a configured 25pt hard SL ($50 cap). MFE = $0 on most of them (no favorable move).
**Diagnosis**: DRM was using `position.AveragePrice` to compute unrealized PnL → maxUnrealizedPts. In Strategy Analyzer multi-TF backtests `Position.AveragePrice` apparently goes stale → DRM sees `maxPts ≈ 0` → Tier1 trail formula yields `entry ± 5pt`. Strategy's own MFE tracker (using `currentEntryPrice`) saw the correct 28pt+ peaks.
**Fix**: pass `currentEntryPrice` and `currentEntryDir` explicitly into `UpdateMaxPnlAndState`, `RouteStopForState`, `CalculateAndRouteStop`. DRM no longer reads `Position.AveragePrice`.
**Status**: shipped to live v1.3-RC.

### 2. v1.0.x: missing exit logic (4-version structural bug exposed)
**Symptom**: `OnHighPivot=Long, OnLowPivot=Long` mode (always-long) generated only 5 trades over 6 days, avg time in market 1,135 min (18.75 hr) — strategy degenerated into "buy at first pivot of session, hold to EOD".
**Diagnosis**: v1.0.0 → v1.0.3 had **no mid-session exit logic**. Exits were an accidental side effect of opposite-direction entries. Counter mode hides this because direction always alternates (HIGH pivot → SHORT entry implicitly exits the prior LONG); same for With mode. Always-long never has the alternation, so the position is trapped until EOD force-close.
**Fix (v1.0.4)**: ALWAYS exit current position before any entry decision. `Skip` action now correctly produces a flat-on-exit. Counter/with modes byte-equivalent (alternation makes the exit-then-same-dir-enter path unreachable). Always-long now properly rebalances at every pivot.
**Status**: shipped to live v1.0.4 (replaces v1.0.3 on Sim101).

## The chop-edge discovery (the big one)

User shared NT8 trade-export `examples/trades.csv` (1,678 trades, 1/2/2026 → 4/24/2026, ~95 trading days). Cumulative PnL trajectory shows a clean inflection on **2026-02-26**:

| Window | Days | Trades | Total | $/day | $/trade |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| WORKING (1/2 → 2/26) | 46 | 714 | **+$4,096** | **+$89** | +$5.74 |
| BLEED (2/27 → 4/24) | 49 | 964 | **−$4,648** | **−$95** | −$4.82 |
| NET | 95 | 1,678 | −$552 | −$5.81 | −$0.33 |

Per-window features:
- **Working**: mean efficiency 0.437 (chop), mean range 408 pts (smaller days)
- **Bleed**: mean efficiency 0.491 (more directional), mean range 469 pts (bigger days)

User's framing: "trend days are the easy ones — chop is what everyone avoids and we thrive."

Per-day quartile validation (more nuanced than pure chop=win):

| Efficiency quartile | Days | Total | Mean/day |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 chop (eff 0.00-0.24) | 24 | −$96 | −$4 |
| Q2 (0.24-0.49) | 24 | +$378 | +$16 |
| **Q3 (0.51-0.64)** | 23 | **−$1,298** | **−$56** |
| Q4 trend (0.65-0.96) | 24 | +$464 | +$19 |

The **mid-efficiency** zone (Q3) is the killer — strategy enters on R-retracements that turn out to be brief pauses, then gets caught when the moderate trend resumes.

| Range-expansion quartile | Days | Total | Mean/day |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Q1 compressed (0.26-0.66× of 20d mean)** | 22 | **−$3,215** | **−$146** |
| Q2 (0.69-0.93×) | 21 | +$138 | +$7 |
| Q3 (0.96-1.27×) | 21 | +$729 | +$35 |
| **Q4 expanded (1.27-2.60×)** | 21 | **+$2,019** | **+$96** |

**The dominant signal is range_expansion**: when today is materially quieter than recent average, friction wins; when today is bigger than usual, the strategy thrives. Compressed-range days (Q1) account for −$3,215 of the bleed.

Combined: strategy wants **above-average range AND chop-or-trend (avoid the moderate Q3 mid-efficiency zone)**.

## Hour-of-day signal (also huge)

Same 1,678 trades aggregated by entry hour (NT8 timestamps in CT, given missing 14-15 hours = MNQ 4-5 PM ET maintenance break):

| Hour bucket | Total | What's happening |
|---|---|---|
| **Hr 6** (7 AM ET) | **−$4,572** | US morning open, news-driven trends |
| Hr 10-11 | −$3,206 | US morning/midday continuation |
| **Hr 12** (1 PM ET) | **+$3,831** | post-lunch consolidation = mean-reverting |
| **Hr 13** (2 PM ET) | **+$3,665** (69% WR) | mid-afternoon US session |
| Hr 4 | +$2,947 | overnight Asia → London transition |

Filter scenario: **trade only the 10 profitable hours** (0, 2, 4, 7, 12, 13, 18, 19, 20, 23) → 666 trades = **+$14,337 instead of −$552 unfiltered**. $15k swing on the same entries by gating on hour. Day-of-week add-on: **Thursday/Friday account for −$5,563 of the bleed**.
