## Methodology

**Pruning strategy** (combinatorial control):
- Layer 1 produces a feature shortlist ranked by Cohen-d (separating regimes) or |corr_lookback_return| (recent-move correlation)
- Top-K (K=10–20) shortlist feeds layers 2/B1/B2/chord
- Layer 2 pairs use 3 quantiles per feature → 9 cells per pair (190 pairs at K=20)
- Chord uses 3 quantiles per feature → 27 cells per triplet (120 triplets at K=10)
- Min cell support = 50 bars to filter noise

**Why descriptive not predictive**: every prior fitted model (Analysis L, MA alignment, single-horizon composite, quantile composite) showed strong regime-conditional behavior. Before fitting more models, characterize the underlying feature behavior so any future model has principled feature selection. The chord/cell mappings ARE the substrate for regime-conditional strategy selection.

## Layer A2 (pair, current) — `tools/v2_features_pairwise_eda.py`

Bins each feature into Q quantiles, computes per-cell stats per pair. "Interaction RMS" = degree to which pair behavior is non-additive (joint cell ≠ sum of marginals).

Top pairs:
- **L1_5m_price_velocity_1b × L1_5m_body**: WR ranges 42–75% across cells (33pp spread). This pair recovers ~70% direction accuracy from just 2 raw features.
- L1_15m_price_velocity_1b × L1_15m_body: 32–67% WR (35pp spread)
- L1_1h_body × L1_1h_price_velocity_1b: 38–60% (23pp spread, +12.2 interaction RMS — strongest non-additivity)

**Pattern**: velocity × body at the same TF captures "directional bar agreement". When velocity AND body both push the same way, continuation. When they disagree (e.g. velocity up, body down = reversal candle), reversion.

## Layer B1 (single, lookback patterns) — `tools/v2_features_lookback_eda.py`

For each top-K feature × each window N ∈ {3, 6, 12, 24}, detect 6 pattern types: RISING_MONO, FALLING_MONO, SPIKE_UP, SPIKE_DOWN, REVERSAL_UP, REVERSAL_DOWN. Measure forward price reaction at t+12.

Top WR-lift patterns (vs 52.1% baseline):
- L1_5m_price_velocity_1b / 6 / RISING_MONO → **69.6% WR (n=46), fwd +38.1 ticks**
- L1_5m_body / 6 / RISING_MONO → 57.9% WR (n=38), **fwd +41.1 ticks**
- L1_15m_body / 3 / SPIKE_DOWN → 59.9% (n=167), fwd +5 (mean reversion)
- L1_5m_body / 6 / REVERSAL_DOWN → 58.0% (n=224), fwd +11 (failed reversal)

**Pattern**: 6-bar monotonic momentum on 5m bar-shape features = strong continuation when it occurs. Single-bar SPIKE_DOWN on 15m → mean-revert (continuation of pre-existing trend, not the spike direction). High-WR patterns are RARE (0.1% of bars), but when they fire the price reaction is decisive.

## Layer B2 (pair, lookback) — `tools/v2_features_lookback_pair_eda.py`

For each (X, Y) pair × window × 8 joint patterns (BOTH_RISING/FALLING, DIVERGE_X_UP/DOWN, SPIKE_BOTH_UP/DOWN, REVERSAL_AGREE_UP/DOWN). Reuses pattern detectors from B1.

Top combos by WR_lift:
- (1m_velocity_w, 5m_velocity_1b) / 6 / **REVERSAL_AGREE_DOWN** → **71.0% WR (n=31), fwd +21.3 ticks**
- (5m_velocity_w, 1m_velocity_w) / 6 / BOTH_FALLING → 61.7% (n=60), fwd +10.8
- (15m_body, 1h_velocity_1b) / 6 / SPIKE_BOTH_DOWN → 59.9% (n=177), fwd +5.6

**Pattern**: coordinated bearish moves predict POSITIVE forward returns — market over-reacts to coordinated bearish signals then bounces. SPIKE_BOTH_UP at 1h+5m gives reliable +13.7 tick continuation at 56% WR over 200+ events.

## Chord finder (triplet) — `tools/v2_features_chord_eda.py`

User's music metaphor operationalized. Don't ask "do 3 features predict better than 2". Ask "do certain triplets co-activate into a recognizable fingerprint". Score each triplet's chord_purity = mean dominant_regime_pct across its quantile cells.

Top chords by purity (cells map cleanly to one specific regime):

| Triplet | Purity | Best cell mapping |
|---|---:|---|
| L1_4h_body + L1_4h_velocity_1b + L3_4h_z_low_w | **54.6%** | cell(1,2,0) = **100% FLAT_SMOOTH** (n=96, fwd −7.5); cell(2,1,1) = **100% FLAT_CHOPPY** (n=96, fwd −22.3) |
| L3_4h_z_se_w + L1_4h_body + L1_4h_velocity_1b | 54.5% | similar — same cells, different ordering |
| L2_1h_velocity_w + L1_4h_body + L1_4h_velocity_1b | 52.3% | cell(1,1,0) = 86% FLAT_SMOOTH (n=84, fwd +7.1, **WR=70%**); cell(2,2,1) = 80% UP_SMOOTH |

**Pattern**: 4h-TF features dominate purity rankings. The 4h smoothing window encodes the most regime-distinct fingerprints. Different cells of the same triplet reliably differentiate FLAT_SMOOTH (mean_fwd ≈ +10) from FLAT_CHOPPY (mean_fwd ≈ −22). The chord IS the regime classifier — and it's deterministic, no fitting.

Top chord by signal magnitude:
- L2_1h_velocity_w + L2_4h_velocity_w + L2_1D_vol_sigma_w → max-cell fwd return **+45.7 ticks**

## Volume × variation EDA — `tools/v2_features_volume_variation_eda.py`

User: "what about volume and the other features, they must describe something" + "how about high variation in a low volume state?".

The prior layers had ranked by Cohen-d (regime separation) or |corr_lookback|, both of which filter OUT volume features because volume doesn't track price velocity directly — exactly the point. Volume describes activity intensity, not direction.

Tool builds (vol, var) pairs at the same TF for 4 TFs (5m, 15m, 1h, 4h). 72 pairs total. Bins each into 3 quantiles → 9 cells per pair. For each cell: regime distribution, mean fwd return, WR, MFE/MAE.

**LOW_VOL × HIGH_VAR cell** (your fakeout question):

| TF | Vol | Var | n | Regime | mean_fwd | WR |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---:|---:|
| 15m | vol_mean_w | price_sigma_w | 486 | FLAT_CHOPPY (61%) | **+28.7** | 53% |
| 15m | vol_sigma_w | price_sigma_w | 513 | FLAT_CHOPPY (54%) | **+27.3** | 58% |
| 5m | vol_mean_w | swing_noise_w | 606 | FLAT_CHOPPY (62%) | **+23.5** | 57% |

Pattern: low volume + high variation → consistently positive forward return (+13 to +29 ticks). FLAT_CHOPPY days where price varies without volume backing = directional moves without conviction = mean-reversion bounce. The cell describes a real state: "noise dispersion without participation, expect bounce".

**HIGH_VOL × LOW_VAR cell** (compression / absorption):

| TF | Vol | Var | n | Regime | mean_fwd | WR |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---:|---:|
| 4h | vol_mean_w | swing_noise_w | 636 | **FLAT_CHOPPY (70%)** | +9.6 | 55% |
| 4h | vol_sigma_w | swing_noise_w | 636 | **FLAT_CHOPPY (70%)** | +6.1 | **60%** |

70% FLAT_CHOPPY purity at 4h compression cells — strongest single-corner concentration in the entire EDA stack. 60% WR.

**4-corner takeaway** (averaged across 72 pairs):

| Corner | Avg fwd | Avg WR | Top regime |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| LOW_VOL × HIGH_VAR | +3.11 | 52.2% | FLAT_CHOPPY (72/72 pairs) |
| HIGH_VOL × LOW_VAR | +0.66 | 52.6% | FLAT_CHOPPY (66/72) |
| HIGH_VOL × HIGH_VAR | +1.30 | 52.1% | FLAT_CHOPPY (72/72) |
| LOW_VOL × LOW_VAR | -0.00 | 52.0% | FLAT_CHOPPY (69/72) |

All four corners are FLAT_CHOPPY-dominated because FLAT_CHOPPY is 64% of all IS bars (the regime distribution itself biases the readings). The DIFFERENTIATING signal is the forward return: LOW_VOL_HIGH_VAR averages +3.1 ticks while LOW_VOL_LOW_VAR averages 0. The "fakeout state" tends upward; the "dead state" goes nowhere. So volume × variation doesn't carve out new regimes — it carves out **forward-return regimes within the same regime label**.
