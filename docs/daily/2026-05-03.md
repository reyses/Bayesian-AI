## Methodology

**Pruning strategy** (combinatorial control):
- Layer 1 produces a feature shortlist ranked by Cohen-d (separating regimes) or |corr_lookback_return| (recent-move correlation)
- Top-K (K=10–20) shortlist feeds layers 2/B1/B2/chord
- Layer 2 pairs use 3 quantiles per feature → 9 cells per pair (190 pairs at K=20)
- Chord uses 3 quantiles per feature → 27 cells per triplet (120 triplets at K=10)
- Min cell support = 50 bars to filter noise

**Why descriptive not predictive**: every prior fitted model (Analysis L, MA alignment, single-horizon composite, quantile composite) showed strong regime-conditional behavior. Before fitting more models, characterize the underlying feature behavior so any future model has principled feature selection. The chord/cell mappings ARE the substrate for regime-conditional strategy selection.

## Contextualization — `tools/v2_features_context_eda.py`

User direction: "there might be features that do not directly relate to price but they might contextualize other features".

For each (modifier, target, TF): bin modifier into quantiles; within each bin, compute target's correlation with forward return. Pair score = range of target_corr_fwd values across modifier bins. High score = modifier MEANINGFULLY changes the target's price relationship.

### Top contextualizers (corr_fwd ranges across modifier quantiles)

| Modifier | Target | TF | Corr_fwd range | Lift |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| **vol_velocity_w** | price_accel_w | 1h | [−0.034, +0.127] | **+0.16** |
| **vol_sigma_w** | price_accel_w | 1h | [−0.070, +0.083] | +0.15 |
| **vol_sigma_w** | body | 1h | [−0.108, +0.039] | +0.15 |
| **vol_velocity_w** | price_velocity_w | 1h | [−0.055, +0.089] | +0.14 |
| **hurst_w** | price_velocity_w | 15m | [−0.059, +0.078] | +0.14 |
| **swing_noise_w** | body | 1h | [−0.087, +0.048] | +0.13 |
| **vol_accel_w** | price_accel_w | 1h | [−0.048, +0.085] | +0.13 |

### Interpretation

When 1h `vol_sigma_w` is in its LOW quantile, `body` has correlation **−0.108** with forward return (negative — body of the bar predicts opposite move). In its HIGH quantile, correlation flips to **+0.039** (essentially zero). Same target, flipped polarity depending on modifier's regime.

The modifier is not signal itself — it's the **conditioning context**. Volume volatility doesn't predict price; it tells us whether body is a continuation signal (low vol_sigma) or a noise signal (high vol_sigma).

### Pattern reads

- **1h-TF modifiers are the strongest contextualizers.** The 1h smoothing window is where contextualization lives. 5m and 15m modifiers exist (hurst_w at 15m has +0.14 lift on price_velocity_w) but are weaker.
- **Volume features dominate as modifiers** — vol_velocity_w, vol_sigma_w, vol_accel_w all appear in the top 10. Volume contextualizes what price moves MEAN.
- **Hurst and swing_noise also work as modifiers**. Hurst tells us trend vs. mean-revert regime; swing_noise tells us range-extension vs. compression.

### Operational implication

A composite signal CAN'T be an additive sum of features. The `body` signal at 1h FLIPS sign depending on `vol_sigma_w`'s quantile. A model that averages them loses this. The right composite framework is **conditional**:

```
if modifier_quantile in (Q0, Q1):  use target's signal as-is
if modifier_quantile in (Q2, Q3):  flip / discount target's signal
```

This is the structure of a regime-router at the feature-pair level. Different from the day-level regime-router (which routes between strategies based on regime_2d). The contextualizer-router operates intra-bar based on modifier quantile.

### Step 2 — Within-day persistence (`tools/v2_features_persistence_eda.py`)

For each cell, compute (a) `P(Q4 at t+k | Q4 at t)` for k = 1..20 (autocorrelation of feature state), and (b) mean_fwd by run-position (bar_1, bar_2, bar_3-4, bar_5-8, bar_9-16, bar_17+) within consecutive Q4 runs.

- **1h-window features are mechanically sticky** (1h column barely updates within a 5m base bar — autocorr at lag-10 > 0.97 is artefact of construction). Real signal lives in 1m/5m feature autocorr.
- **Forward-return decay is mostly DISTRIBUTED**, not front-loaded. `vol_mean_w_5m DOWN_SMOOTH`: bar_1 −68 / bar_5-8 −24 / bar_17+ −53 — U-shape: mid-run weakens, very long Q4 runs reappear with strong signal (regime-transition mechanic).
- Few features show clean front-loading; the entry-bar advantage exists but the held-position edge degrades only modestly through the run.
- Output: `reports/findings/v2_features_persistence/` (4800 autocorr cells, 10 plots).

### Step 3 — Lead-lag analysis (`tools/v2_features_leadlag_eda.py`)

For each cell, compute Pearson corr between feature_t and return over [t+s, t+s+window] for s in {−12, −8, −4, −2, −1, 0, +1, +2, +4, +8, +12} bars. Peak shift tells us whether feature LEADS price (s>0), is CONTEMPORANEOUS (s=0), or LAGS (s<0).

- **89.7% of cells LAG** (peak |corr| at s<0). Only 8.8% lead, 1.5% contemporaneous.
- `price_velocity_w_5m`: peak corr **+0.90 at s=−12** across ALL six regimes. Pure momentum confirmation, not prediction.
- The 12 genuine LEADERS with |corr| > 0.05:
  - `bar_range_1h UP_CHOPPY` s=+12 corr=+0.19 (strongest)
  - `vol_mean_w_1h DOWN_SMOOTH` s=+12 corr=+0.10
  - `hurst_w_1h UP_SMOOTH` s=+12 corr=+0.07
  - `hurst_w` appears 4× in the leading list — **structure features lead, velocity lags**.
- This re-frames every prior layer: most of the regime-conditional signal we celebrated was lag artefact (feature reflects what JUST happened to price). True predictive content is thin and lives in 1h-window structure features.
- Output: `reports/findings/v2_features_leadlag/` (3630 cells, 15 plots).

### Step 4 — Tail asymmetry (`tools/v2_features_tail_asymmetry_eda.py`)

For each cell, compare Q0 (bottom quintile) vs Q4 (top quintile) of feature distribution: magnitude ratio, dispersion ratio, sign flip.

- **75% of cells are SAME-SIGN BOTH TAILS.** Within a trending regime, both extremes push price the same direction.
  - `bar_range_1m DOWN_SMOOTH`: Q0 −7, Q4 −45 (both bearish)
  - `price_sigma_w_5s UP_SMOOTH`: Q0 +7, Q4 +49 (both bullish)
  - `swing_noise_w_1m DOWN_SMOOTH`: Q0 −6, Q4 −44 (both bearish)
- Only **10.7%** are symmetric_opposite (textbook low→bearish, high→bullish).
- The 20 one-sided trigger cells **all live in FLAT regimes** (FLAT_CHOPPY 13, FLAT_SMOOTH 7) — no regime direction, so only one tail carries directional signal (e.g. `bar_range_1h FLAT_CHOPPY` Q4 = +3.8, Q0 = −0.9).
- **Interpretation**: features are MAGNITUDE scalars, not direction predictors. Regime carries direction. Composite implication: in trending regimes, don't bet feature direction — bet regime direction, scale by feature.
- Output: `reports/findings/v2_features_tail_asymmetry/` (328 cells, 10 plots).

### Step 5 — Regime drift / calendar stability (`tools/v2_features_drift_eda.py`)

Split IS into early (Jan-May 2025, 104 days) and late (May-Sep 2025, 104 days) halves. For each cell, measure Q4 mean_fwd in each half + corr(feature, fwd) in each half + sign flip rate.

- **Q4 mean sign-flip rate: 7.0% (23/328 cells)**. All flips concentrate in CHOPPY/FLAT regimes:
  - `price_sigma_w_1h UP_CHOPPY`: early +37.6 → late −4.1 (BIG flip)
  - `price_sigma_w_15m DOWN_CHOPPY`: −31.5 → +13.0
  - `vol_mean_w_1h DOWN_CHOPPY`: −24.0 → +5.8
- **Correlation sign-flip rate: 14.9% (49/328)** — higher than mean-flip rate. Often the Q4 mean stays stable while the overall correlation flips:
  - `price_velocity_w_5m DOWN_SMOOTH`: corr +0.03 → −0.19, but Q4 mean stayed −33 to −39.
- **Most-stable high-magnitude cells (82 total) are dominated by UP_SMOOTH/DOWN_SMOOTH**:
  - `bar_range_5m UP_SMOOTH`: 44.78 → 40.95 (stab 0.91)
  - `swing_noise_w_5s UP_SMOOTH`: 46.40 → 48.86 (stab 0.95)
  - `price_sigma_w_5s UP_SMOOTH`: 47.34 → 51.28 (stab 0.92)
- **Bottom line**: SMOOTH-regime Q4 signals are robust; CHOPPY/FLAT relationships drift. Future composite work should weight by regime-specific stability.
- Output: `reports/findings/v2_features_drift/` (328 cells, 10 plots).

### Drill-down synthesis (5-step takeaways together)

1. **Regime carries direction; feature carries amplitude.** Tail asymmetry shows 75% of cells are same-sign both tails — magnitude not direction. Lead-lag shows 89.7% lag — momentum confirmation not prediction.
2. **The thin slice of genuine leading content lives in 1h-window structure features** (hurst_w, bar_range_1h, vol_mean_w_1h). Velocity/sigma features are confirmation indicators.
3. **Signals are RTH-localized.** All extreme regime cells fire in us_pre / us_open / us_morning / us_lunch. Pre-market and asia_close are flat. The previously-reported magnitudes were averages diluted across silent hours.
4. **SMOOTH > CHOPPY for stability.** SMOOTH-regime Q4 signals replicate across IS halves; CHOPPY relationships drift in both mean and correlation.
5. **Q4 signal persistence is DISTRIBUTED across run positions** at lower TFs, not front-loaded. Holding into a Q4-state run gives most of the entry-bar edge for several bars (decays slowly, recovers at very long runs).

### Headline architectural findings

1. **Feature redundancy families** (D1): 23 v2 features collapse to ~7 independent axes. `vwap_w` and `price_mean_w` are literal duplicates (r=1.000). Dispersion, volume-kinetic, bar-direction, z-score, and volume-state form internal cliques.

2. **Asymmetric volatility through the v2 substrate** (D2): the same `price_velocity_w × price_sigma_w` correlation has opposite sign in UP-trends vs DOWN-trends. Universal Pearson on the full IS (+0.85) hides two opposite regimes. 577 such sign-flip pairs.

3. **z_se_w is the universal contextualizer** (D8): regression dislocation modifies more pair correlations than any other feature. `bar_range × body` correlation goes from −0.90 (Z=Q0) to +0.90 (Z=Q4). Physical reading: in negative-z territory big bars are DOWN moves; in positive-z big bars are UP moves.

4. **Feature×feature OOS survives at 95–100%** (D9), vs feature×price 25.8% in Layer C1. Reason: feature relationships are measured on the same bars at the same time, no time-asymmetric leakage. **Conditional structure is mechanical microstructure, not noise concentration.**

5. **Composite signals can't be additive** — confirmed empirically. 18.7% of D8 triplets show Z-bin literally flipping the sign of corr(X,Y). The right composite is regime-routered, not summed. (User's framework hypothesis from earlier in session, now empirically validated.)

### Operational implication — strategy reframe

Don't try to predict price directly with a feature composite. Instead:

1. Use surviving D7+D8 rules as a **regime DETECTOR** at each bar (95-100% OOS-stable).
2. Map detected regime → direction via regime baselines (UP_SMOOTH +20 ticks/1h, DOWN_SMOOTH −22 ticks/1h, etc.).
3. Use regime-conditional feature triplets from C1 OOS-survivors (31 cells) as confirmation gates within the detected regime.

This is the cleanest synthesis of the full session's work: feature×feature gives regime, regime gives direction, regime-conditional triplets give entry timing. The substrate is now empirically grounded.
