## 3. STRATEGY REFRAME (user, this session) — roles INVERTED
Previously I treated z-extreme as the trigger and 5m as a gate. User corrected:
> "we only use the Z to get a better idea on where we are located, so if mean regression
> is going up then we enter when we are below the mean to get best price."

- **5m mean slope = the SIGNAL/direction.** Slope up → go LONG (trade WITH the trend);
  slope down → SHORT. This is **trend-following with a pullback entry**, NOT mean reversion.
- **z = LOCATION only** (times entry AND exit for a good price): uptrend → wait for z below the
  mean (a dip) before going long; downtrend → wait for z above the mean (a rip) before shorting.
- This **invalidates §2's "with-trend lost −$1.58"** for the new strategy — that number came from
  a z-revert-to-mean exit, which caps a trend winner at the mean and never lets the trend pay.

### Chosen design (user pick) — NEXT TO BUILD
- **Entry**: 5m slope up + z at a dip (z ≤ −Z_ENTRY) → LONG; 5m slope down + z at a rip
  (z ≥ +Z_ENTRY) → SHORT. (z times the entry = best price in the trend direction.)
- **Exit**: **on 5m trend flip** (5m slope crosses through zero), with **z used to time** the
  exit (location, never direction). Pure trend ride; longest holds, fewest trades.
- Build `research/nmp_trend_pullback.py`; same Feb-2024 IS harness + day-block CI; then OOS
  (other 2024 months) + costs.
