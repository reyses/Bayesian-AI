## 4. `research/recovery_dynamics/` — the oscillation FRAMEWORK (the big arc)
Co-designed with Moises. All PURE MEASUREMENT (no trading). Decomposed MNQ price into a **fixed period clock + a breathing amplitude knob + a stable trend rate**.
- **opportunity_cost** — holding a wrong trade to breakeven costs ~2 trades / ~6 min (mode); bimodal, cost is in the tail + never-recovers.
- **anchor_period** (cleanest): first-return time to an every-bar anchor = the oscillation period. **mode 2-3m, 71% revert <15m, ~7.2% trend (no-return)**. **STABLE across 2024/2025 to <0.3%.** This CORRECTED an earlier over-claim of a "non-stationary clock (27 vs 15m)" — that was an artifact of conditioning on a fixed 5-pt drawdown (regime-sensitive selection). Period is a market CONSTANT.
- **period × amplitude decomposition**: period & amplitude tightly coupled; **super-diffusive** (amp/√period rises → momentum in long cycles). **DECOMPOSITION: period = timing constant; amplitude = the regime (2025 30-50% wider at every period).** "A fixed clock with a volume knob."
- **amplitude_evolution** (PNG): the volume knob breathes **~4× (5.5pt→20pt)** over 2024→2025, slow/persistent (causally estimable); trend share comparatively stable ~6-8%.
- **anchor_drift_conditional**: price returns to the anchor EVEN WHILE TRENDING — return rate 93.7% (calm) → **91.1% at max drift**, period 5m at EVERY drift level. So the every-bar anchor already contains the within-trend zigzag; the 7% no-return = the trend's abandoned trailing levels (footprint), NOT "trending time." (My `oscillation_detrended.py` MA version got a consistent answer but was an unneeded parameter.)
- **oscillator_vs_trend / kicker**: 91% oscillation (wash) / 9% symmetric trend tail (49/51 win/loss); after a wrong trade returns to zero, 52% make a favorable 2nd leg (median 18pt, A2/A1=1.13 symmetric). Edge = asymmetric handling of the tail (ride winner / cut adverse) = the R-trigger/B9/λ stack, re-derived from first principles.
