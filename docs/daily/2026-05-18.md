## 1m CSV authoritative — bad-tick filtering discovered

User pointed out we have raw 1m CSVs from NT8 (58 days, full coverage).
Re-converted all 1m CSVs to parquet (overwrite). Re-ran validation.

### Remaining 1m mismatch on 7 days = NT8 bad-tick filtering

Investigated 2026_03_13 specifically:
  - 1m CSV last minute (ts=1773431999): low=24600.50, close=24608.75, vol=213
  - 1m rebin from 1s same minute: low=24549.50, close=24549.50, vol=216
  - Diff: $59.25 close, +3 vol

Dug into the 1s data for that minute (27 bars):
  - 26 bars: prices 24600-24615 (range $15)
  - 1 spurious bar at ts=1773431999: low=24549.50, vol=19 contracts
  - That's a **$58 spike** on 19 contracts when surrounding trades are
    $24604-24608 — a CLASSIC BAD TICK

**Conclusion**: NT8's 1m aggregator includes bad-tick filtering that
removes spikes. The 1s stream is raw; the 1m CSV is filtered. For
the 7 days with previous OHLC mismatch:
  - 1m CSV (now in parquet) is AUTHORITATIVE
  - 1s data contains the raw spike ticks
  - My 1s-rebin captured the spike (= "wrong" for analytical purposes)

### Operational implication

The hardened forward pass walks 5s closes. 5s = rebinned from 1s,
which means it INCLUDES the bad ticks. On the 7 affected days, this
could trigger spurious R-trigger fires for ~1 bar per day.

Impact: tiny. 7 bars × $58 impact / ~17k legs total = <0.005% of
total trade volume affected. Forward pass results from the morning
remain valid.

For future: if we want clean 5s data, apply a tick-spike filter
before rebinning (e.g., reject 1s bars where high-low > 3x median).
Or accept the noise.

### Key methodological lessons (saved to memory)

1. ACTION SURFACE > prediction quality. High AUC means signal exists;
   the action needs to MATCH the signal direction.

   vol-loving strategies. Defend low-vol, attack high-vol.
