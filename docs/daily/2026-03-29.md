## EDA Findings (Step 3b)
### E2: Features at Level Boundaries
- **z_se (p=5.6e-18)**: THE level feature — z-score extremes = hand-drawn levels
- **std_price (p=2.9e-27)**: volatility DROPS at levels (compression)
- **vwap_distance (p=4.8e-12)**: price far from VWAP at levels
- **dmi_diff (p=9.9e-10)**: DMI positive at boundaries
- **bar_range (p=3.4e-15)**: bars get BIGGER at boundaries

### E3: Reversal vs Breakout
- **wick_ratio (p=0.019)**: smaller wicks = reversal, bigger wicks = breakout
- **dmi_gap (p=0.048)**: lower trend strength = reversal, higher = breakout

### E4: Cross-TF Correlation
- 1h→1m: most features INDEPENDENT (<0.20) — each TF adds unique info
- 15m bridges 1h and 1m (0.37-0.73 range)
- bar_range most correlated across TFs (0.58-0.75)
- time_of_day redundant (obviously)

### E5: Lead/Lag
- **1m LEADS 1h for**: DMI, gap, volume, z_se (direction features)
- **1h LEADS 1m for**: velocity, acceleration (speed features)
- Micro detects direction change first. Macro detects momentum first.

### E7: Feature-Level Interaction
- z_se (-0.154) = most level-aware feature
- vwap_distance (-0.132) = second
- dmi_diff (-0.122) = third

## Shape Analysis (Step 3c)
- 2,588 level touch events classified: reversal (1031), breakout (1058), plateau (166), bounce (333)
- Shapes are VISUALLY DISTINCT per type
- Feature profiles show: bar_range and wick_ratio diverge BEFORE the touch = PREDICTIVE
- Reversal: z_se drops at touch, DMI turns negative, V-shape
- Breakout: small bars before, then big bars through. Same approach as reversal but continues
- Bounce: approach from above, big rejection bar
