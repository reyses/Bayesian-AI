## Summary
Built DualHeadPredictor (shared backbone, state head + direction head). Trained per TF independently. 1h passed gate at 95.4% (t+1, epoch-based). 1m failed at t+10 (83%), passed at t+4 (96.3%, walk-forward). Key insight: t+4 works because it predicts TO the oscillation peak, not past it. Forward horizon = half the oscillation period, not a tuning parameter.

## Key Insights
- **Oscillation half-cycle is the forward horizon**: t+4 at 1m works because the natural oscillation period is ~8 bars. Predicting t+4 = predicting to the peak. t+10 = predicting past the peak into the next cycle = noise.
- **1-bar lookahead is sufficient**: Instead of predicting N bars ahead, just predict "will the next bar continue this direction?" The oscillation emerges from the sequence of 1-bar predictions: up, up, up, up, DOWN → hold 4 bars then exit.
- **Epoch-based overfits 1m**: Best epoch was 5 on 350K bars. Walk-forward (daily carry-forward) is correct for 1m (1,380 bars/day = enough data).
- **Epoch-based works for 1h**: Only 5.8K train bars, can't overfit. Epoch-based + held-out 2026 validation.
- **Dual head reinforces**: State head (MSE) forces physics understanding. Direction head (BCE) forces binary correctness. At 1h both heads agree 98% and accuracy rises. DIR_LOSS_WEIGHT=0.1 best for 1m (physics dominates).
- **1s needs sharded processing**: 15M bars crashes single-pass. Monthly shards (~1M each) through SFE, cached to disk, walk-forward across months.

## Future: Probabilistic Conviction Decay Curve
Multi-horizon P(long) per bar — the model predicts n+1 through n+K simultaneously.
The decay shape IS the trade signal:

```
Bar 100: n+1=95% n+2=88% n+3=72% n+4=55%  → "3 bars of runway"
Bar 101: n+1=92% n+2=85% n+3=68% n+4=51%  → "still 3 bars"
Bar 102: n+1=87% n+2=60% n+3=45%           → "only 1 bar left"
Bar 103: n+1=48%                             → RETREAT
```

Key principles:
- **Decision is ALWAYS n+1** — "will the next bar continue?" Act only on this.
- **Curve is CONTEXT** — n+2 through n+K measure runway remaining.
- **Recalculated every bar** — not a fixed prediction, a rolling assessment.
- **Steep decay** = near oscillation peak, exit soon.
- **Flat decay** = strong trend, hold through oscillation dips.
- **n+1 flips below 50%** = next bar goes against you = RETREAT signal.
- When n+1 through n+4 all agree = high conviction. When n+1 says long but n+2 says short = last bar of the move.
- No fixed hold duration. No fixed SL. The physics decide.
- The "lookahead" misses (peaks/troughs) are where the curve is steepest — the model knows it's uncertain there.

This replaces: L2 (duration), L3 (retreat), fixed exits. One model, one output (decay curve), all decisions derived.

## Late Session: Trajectory Navigation Architecture
- **All three models pass**: 1h (95.4%), 1m (96.3%), 1s (96.6%) at top 5% confidence
- **Statistical significance**: P-values effectively zero, CI never includes 50%
- **Binomial logistic regression** calibrates P(D) to actual outcomes
- **1h chop zone**: 50-62%, **1m chop zone**: ~42-50%
- **Three regimes**: trending (P(D) consistently above chop), inflection (P(D) rapidly crossing 50%), chop (P(D) stuck near 50%)
- **Key distinction**: P(D)=0.48 after being 0.92 = INFLECTION (exit). P(D)=0.48 after being 0.51 = CHOP (no trade)
- **Architecture**: 1h=WHERE we're going, 1m=WAVES along the way, 1s=WHEN to step in
