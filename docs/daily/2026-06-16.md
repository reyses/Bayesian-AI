# 2026-06-16: Genetic Kalman CA Tuning & OOS Validation

## 1. Thesis & Objective
The prior 7.5m cubic regression was too noisy at the endpoint (378 turns/day), causing the engine to clip massive peaks during exits. The goal was to transition to a true causal Kalman Constant-Acceleration filter and genetically tune it across H1 2024 to capture the entire macro move without whipsawing.

## 2. What We Accomplished
- **JIT Compilation**: Rewrote the 3x3 Kalman matrix filter natively in Numba. This allowed us to evaluate 6 full months of 1-second ticks in under 1 second per pass, unlocking true Genetic Algorithm (GA) capability.
- **Genetic Tuning**: Ran `scipy.optimize.differential_evolution` across H1 2024 (In-Sample). The GA optimized `Q_JERK`, `R_MEAS`, entry velocity thresholds, and the exit trailing drawdown.
- **IS/OOS Validation**: Pushed the optimally tuned Kalman engine across all 604 days (2024-2026) using strict MNQ $2/pt costing to verify robustness.
- **GIF Generation**: Generated a causal `matplotlib.animation` showing the heavily smoothed Kalman line tracking a winning trade.

## 3. Metrics & Discoveries
- **Optimal Kalman Dials**: The GA preferred a *massively* smoothed line (`Q_JERK = 1.81e-09`, `R_MEAS = 12.55`), effectively turning the Kalman filter into an iron-clad macro anchor.
- **The Entry Gate**: It demanded an explosive `0.066 pts/sec` (~4 pts/min) velocity to confirm a true trend before firing the entry.
- **The Exit Logic**: The GA completely abandoned flipping the vector to exit. Instead, it determined that trailing the peak MFE by a massive `79.4 points` ($158) was the optimal way to let the wave breathe.
- **Average MFE Consistency**: Across IS, OOS-1, and OOS-2, the average MFE remained locked at **~71 points** per trade. 
- **OOS Degradation**: While IS generated $7.4k, OOS-2 (2025-26) degraded to +$478 net. The engine fired 3,694 times in 2025-26 (vs 630 in H1 2024), destroying gross PnL via friction. Market dynamics shifted to a much choppier environment in 25-26.

## 4. Next Steps & Handoff
- The massive 79-point trailing stop is highly capital intensive (huge open risk). We need to explore if we can build a more intelligent causal exit (like the R2 collapse EDA) to lock in those 71-point MFEs earlier without relying on a dumb trail.
- The massive jump in trade frequency during 2025-2026 suggests our entry condition isn't adapting to varying volatility regimes. We should test normalizing the entry velocity threshold against the recent ATR.
