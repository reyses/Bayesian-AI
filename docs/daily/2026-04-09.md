## Research: Additional Entry Physics Candidates
From web research — trading concepts that translate to 79D features:

### 1. EXHAUSTION BAR (Volume Climax Reversal)
Largest bar in the move + no follow-through after impulsive trend.
- `1m_bar_range` = highest in recent window (climax bar)
- `1m_velocity` decelerating (follow-through dying)
- `5m_velocity` still extreme (impulsive move preceded it)
- Like PEAK but driven by bar_range climax instead of DMI
- Sources: VSA climactic bars, NQ reversal framework

### 2. ABSORPTION (Large Volume, No Price Movement)
Huge volume but price doesn't move = big player absorbing orders.
- `1m_vol_rel` very high (volume spike)
- `1m_bar_range` very low (price didn't move despite volume)
- `1m_wick_ratio` high (rejection wicks)
- New signal: volume/range divergence
- Sources: Footprint charts, order flow analysis

### 3. DELTA DIVERGENCE (Price vs Volume)
Price makes new extreme but volume/direction weakening.
- `1m_z_se` making new extreme
- `1m_vol_rel` dropping (volume drying)
- `1m_dmi_diff` diverging from z
- Partially covered by PEAK already
- Sources: CVD divergence, order flow patterns

### 4. VARIANCE RATIO REGIME FLIP
vr crosses from trending (>1) to mean-reverting (<1).
- `1m_variance_ratio` dropping below 1.0 from above
- Combined with `1m_hurst` dropping below 0.5
- Regime change entry, not level entry
- Sources: Hurst exponent research, stat arb literature

### 5. MULTI-TF MOMENTUM EXHAUSTION
Lower TFs exhaust while higher TFs still push.
- `1m_velocity` near zero (exhausted at micro level)
- `5m_velocity` still extreme (macro still pushing)
- Bounce coming when micro exhausts against macro trend
- Sources: Multi-timeframe analysis
