# 2026-05-22 — Trade Outcome Probability Table (continuation + recovery diagnostics)

## Request
User: "create a probability table of all the trades from 0 to max price ...
entry to close, MAE, MFE." Sharpened in a follow-up to two **conditional**
questions:
1. **Continuation** — given a trade is currently up `+x`, how likely to keep
   running and close beyond `+$200`?
2. **Recovery** — given a trade is currently down `-d`, how likely to still
   "work out in the end" (close net-positive)?

A diagnostic asset, not a trading model — consistent with the 2026-05-21
reframe. No production code touched.

## Findings (17,767 IS legs / 275 days; 2,936 OOS legs / 51 days)
- **Continuation**: `P(close≥$200 | MFE≥x)` rises 2.4%(x=0) → ~25%(x≈4R) →
  ~85%(x≈8R) IS; OOS tracks it closely. A big close requires a big peak —
  there is no edge in "it's already green".
- **Structural ~1R giveback confirmed**: `P(close≥x) holds` (ends at or above
  the level it reached) sits flat at ~44-50% across all x — once a leg reaches
  `+x` it ends at-or-above `+x` only about half the time. `close ≈ MFE − R`.
- **Recovery is the headline answer**: `P(close>0 | MAE≥d)` falls 42.7% →
  25.6% by d=0.75R IS (41.5% → 21% OOS), then **survivorship-rebounds** to
  ~38% at d≥1.25R (deep-drawdown legs still open are early entries into big
  moves). BUT **mean close is negative for every drawdown bucket ≥0.5R** in
  both IS and OOS — in expectation a leg that has drawn down ≥0.5R is a net
  loser even though it sometimes recovers.
- **Joint 2D**: `P(close>0)` is almost entirely MFE-driven — ~0% at MFE<1R,
  ~95%+ at MFE≥2R; MAE only erodes it at the extreme corner.
- Ordering: 99-100% of recovered legs dipped before they rallied (mechanical).

## Read for the user's two questions
1. *Continuing beyond +$200* — the odds are basically the MFE distribution
   shifted down ~1R; reaching a +4R peak ≈ 25% chance of a ≥$200 close, +6R ≈
   53%. "Currently green" alone carries little information beyond how high.
2. *Going negative and working out* — **no, in expectation**. Recovery rate is
   ~21-26% in the shallow-drawdown zone and mean close is negative for every
   d≥0.5R. The deep-d rebound is survivorship, not a hold signal. This
   re-confirms `drawdown_stop_analysis` — "hold the loser, it'll come back" is
   not supported by the data.

## Session continued — full conditional exploration + consolidation

The session went well beyond the first tool. The user drilled through a long
series of conditional questions; key results (all now in the suite below):
- **Cut-and-bank a winner loses** — hold−cut EV positive at every +$L level
  (heavy right tail pays for the giveback).
- **Giveback is a fixed ~1R toll** — eats $50-100 peaks whole, minor on $300+.
- **From a +$300 peak** the close clusters in $200-300; below $0 is ~1% — a
  great excursion does NOT "give it all back".
- **Equity loss is a low-MFE phenomenon** — by +$150 MFE P(close<0)≈1%; a
  "$250 safety limit" guards empty space. Equity losses are trades that never
  worked → an entry-quality problem, not an exit-safety one.
- **Cutting/bailing a loser loses** at every drawdown level — the R-trigger
  recovers ~1R off the low; bailing forfeits it.
- **"Drawdown gains momentum" does NOT replicate** — P(deepen) rises IS, falls
  OOS over the same well-populated range.
- **Trade AGE beats depth** — recoverers run ~2× longer than non-recoverers
  (matches the existing `leg_age_hazard` hump finding).
- **Bimodal MAE-timing = winners vs losers**, not "peak then collapse" — the
  "peak" in the losing mode is ~$14 (noise, not a peak).
- Through-line: the R-trigger reversal exit is an adaptive stop; no fixed-dollar
  cut/lock/bail overlay beats it on EV.

### THE STACK DOESN'T TRANSFER FROM DATABENTO TO NT8

Reran `forward_zigzag` both ways under the corrected split:

|                            | IS (Databento)     | OOS (NT8)         |
|----------------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| `causal_flat` (no GBMs)    | −$332/day (14,274 legs) | −$175/day (2,127 legs) |
| `causal` (B7+B9+B10 on)    | −$214/day (9,829 legs)  | −$185/day (1,822 legs) |
| **stack lift**             | **+$118/day**       | **−$10/day**       |

Headline: **the L5 filter stack (B7+B9+B10) adds +$118/day on Databento IS
but is a wash (−$10/day) on NT8 OOS.** B7 skips ~31% of Databento legs to
positive effect; on the cleaner NT8 tape the same filter skips ~14% of legs
and the net is neutral-to-slight-loss.

Two readings (both plausible, can't fully disentangle without head-to-head
days):
1. NT8 has built-in bad-tick filtering → fewer raw whipsaws for B7 to
   discriminate against → fewer "obvious losers" to skip.
2. The stack is *data-coupled to Databento* (trained on Databento; its
   discrimination doesn't carry to the NT8 distribution).

**Implication: the user's hypothesis "B1–B8 are the live fix" — tested on the
L5 subset, FAILS on NT8.** The filters lift the noisier Databento sample but
do not transfer to the cleaner NT8 tape that live actually trades. Live
(NT8) causal expectation stays **~−$150 to −$185/day**, with or without the
existing stack.
